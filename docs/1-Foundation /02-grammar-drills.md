# Grammar Drills — 8 Rules + Corrections

> **How to use:** Don't read. Say aloud. One rule per Day 1 and Day 3 session block.
> Apply every rule to sentences from `06-automation-context.md` — never generic examples.

---

## Rule 1 — The Two-Second Rule
**Problem:** Chaining sentences with "and / so / which is" sounds uncertain and junior.
**Fix:** Say one sentence. Stop. Pause two seconds in silence. Start the next sentence.

**Wrong:** "I built the webhook and it connects to n8n and then it fires the AI classification which is it sends the email."
**Right:** "I built the webhook. It connects to n8n. When a lead comes in, the AI classifies them. The email goes out within 60 seconds."

Practice: Take any 3-sentence chain from your notes. Break it into separate statements. Say each one with a 2-second pause.

---

## Rule 2 — The Tense Anchor
**Problem:** Switching tenses mid-story confuses the listener.
**Fix:** Before you start speaking, pick one time frame — Past, Present, or Future — and lock into it.

- **Past:** "I built the system. I tested it. It worked."
- **Future:** "I'm gonna build the system. I'll test it. Then we go live."
- **Wrong:** "I built the system and then I will test it and it works fine."

Practice: Say the Marcus story (lead response delivery) entirely in past tense. No present-tense verbs.

---

## Rule 3 — The Question Flip
**Problem:** Arabic puts the subject before the auxiliary verb. English reverses it.
**Fix:** Every question needs an auxiliary verb (do/does/is/are/can/will/have) BEFORE the subject.

| Wrong (Arabic order) | Right (English order) |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| "What your current process look like?" | "What does your current process look like?" |
| "How many leads you're getting per month?" | "How many leads are you getting per month?" |
| "Do you think which tool I have to use it?" | "Which tool do you think I should use?" |
| "Will affect me good or not?" | "Will it affect me well?" |
| "How many words a day I have to speak?" | "How many words a day do I have to speak?" |

Practice: Say all 5 audit call discovery questions from `06-automation-context.md`. Check: auxiliary verb before subject, every time.

---

## Rule 4 — The "Which Is" Ban
**Problem:** "which is" is filler. It makes sentences sound incomplete.
**Fix:** Delete it. Replace with a direct word, a simple "is," or a colon.

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "My first goal which is lead response." | "My first goal is lead response." |
| "I know how to fix this, which is I use Claude." | "I know how to fix this: I use Claude." |
| "The system, which is it runs on n8n." | "The system runs on n8n." |

Practice: Scan your last Wispr Flow transcript. Find every "which is." Delete it. Replace using the rule above.

---

## Rule 5 — Input Correction Loop
**Problem:** Repeating the same mistake without noticing.
**Fix:** After any recording, listen back. When you catch an error, say the corrected version 3x immediately. Don't re-listen to the whole thing — focus on catching and correcting specific patterns.

Key patterns to listen for:
- Did any statement rise in pitch at the end?
- Did you chain 3+ clauses with "and/so"?
- Did you switch tenses mid-sentence?
- Did any question have wrong word order?

---

## Rule 6 — The Mirroring Technique
**Problem:** Running out of ways to talk about a topic.
**Fix:** For any topic, generate 10 distinct questions about it. Then answer 3 of them.

Example topic: "Lead Response Automation"
1. "What happens the moment a lead fills your form?"
2. "How long does your current response take?"
3. "What does a missed lead cost you on average?"
4. "Have you tried to automate this before?"
5. "What would a 60-second response time change for your business?"
... (continue to 10)

Then answer 3 as if you're the client.

---

## Rule 7 — Nuance Injection
**Problem:** Robotic or translated phrases break the American professional sound.
**Fix:** Replace common Egyptian-English constructions with natural equivalents.

| Robotic / Translated | Natural American |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| "You understand me" | "You got me" / "Does that make sense?" |
| "I make a mistake" | "I made a mistake" / "My bad" |
| "It will affect me bad" | "It'll work against me" / "That'll hurt" |
| "I feel deep that" | "Deep down I feel" / "I strongly believe" |
| "Hitting two birds" | "Killing two birds with one stone" |
| "Way more better" | "Way better" / "Much better" |

## Core Verbs — Make vs. Do vs. Cause

| Verb | Use it for | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Do** | Actions and tasks | "Do research," "do a test," "do the setup" |
| **Make** | Creating and producing | "Make a plan," "make a decision," "make progress," "make a mistake" |
| **Cause** | Effects and results | "That will cause delays," "this causes fatigue" — NOT "make fatigue" |

## Prepositions — Stop Translating

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| search about | search for |
| compare between A and B | compare A and B |
| look about if | look to see if |
| look to people | look for people |
| based on my knowledge only | based solely on my knowledge |
| make a schedule | create a schedule / set a schedule |
| make them as packages | turn them into packages |
| feel deep that | feel deeply that / deep down |
