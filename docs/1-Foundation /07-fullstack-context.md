> This file is for English speaking practice. Use it for Friday and Saturday sessions.

## The Core Pitch

These two sentences define the full-stack business. Know them cold:

> "We take a business doing things manually or disconnectedly — and we make it run."
> "We don't sell technology. We sell the outcome."

Practice: Say each one 5x. Falling pitch on "run." Falling pitch on "outcome."

## The Pain Map — 6 Patterns You Walk Into

When you talk to a new client, you're walking into one of these six problems. Know how to describe each one:

**01 — Manual Reporting Every Day**
"Someone opens a spreadsheet. They copy numbers. They paste them somewhere else. Every morning. No automation. Pure waste."

**02 — Cold Leads**
"The form was filled. The CRM wasn't updated. The email was never sent. The lead went cold. Revenue disappeared."

**03 — No-Shows Killing You**
"No reminder was sent. It was a manual task. It was forgotten. The clinic. The restaurant. The booking. All gone."

**04 — Data Lives in 5 Places**
"Sheets. Notion. WhatsApp. Email. Somewhere. Nobody has one clear view. Nobody agrees on the numbers."

**05 — The App Idea Stays an Idea**
"Two developers. Two budgets. Two platforms. Too complex. Too expensive. The idea sits on a notes app forever."

**06 — Hours Watching to Stay Relevant**
"No system. No summary. No digest. You open YouTube and an hour disappears. Nothing was captured. You forget."

Practice: Pick any 2 problems. Describe them out loud as if you're on a discovery call. Don't read — describe.

## Discovery Questions for Full-Stack Prospects

Say each 3x — auxiliary verb before subject, every time:

1. "What does your team's daily workflow look like right now?"
2. "Where does the most manual work happen — what are people doing by hand?"
3. "Do you have an app idea that's been sitting on paper for a while?"
4. "How long does it take you to get a business update — revenue, orders, status?"
5. "What would change for you if your team got automated reports every morning?"
6. "Are you losing leads because your response time is too slow?"
7. "What does a no-show cost your business on average?"
8. "Have you tried to build an app before? What stopped you?"
9. "What's one thing your business does manually that you wish just... ran itself?"
10. "If I could eliminate three hours of manual work per day — where would your team use that time?"

## Objections You'll Face — Practice Answering These

**"I already have a developer for this."**
→ "That's fine. The question isn't who builds it — it's how fast you need it running and what it costs to maintain. I deliver in days, not months, and I hand you the keys on day one."

**"Is this going to break?"**
→ "Everything I build includes a health check — it runs every morning. If something breaks, you know before the day starts. Not after a client complains."

**"This feels expensive."**
→ "Let me put it this way. If the automation saves your team 3 hours a day — that's 60 hours a month. What does one hour of your team's time cost? The system pays for itself in the first week."

**"I don't use those tools."**
→ "Tell me what you do use. I'll map it to your stack. The workflow adapts to you — you don't adapt to the workflow."

Practice: Pick any objection. Answer it out loud without reading. Record. Listen back.
