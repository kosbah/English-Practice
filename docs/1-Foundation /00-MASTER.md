## The Tone — One Direction

**You are:** A direct, confident American automation and full-stack professional.
**You sound like:** Someone who knows what they're doing and doesn't waste words.
**You do NOT sound like:** A hustle-bro, a formal corporate executive, or someone translating from Arabic.

The target is already in your hands. These 10 sentences — from your real business — are your north star. Say any of them right, and you're there:

1. "The moment a lead fills your form, the system fires automatically — no manual step."
2. "I classify every lead as Hot, Warm, or Cold — so your team knows who to call first."
3. "The follow-up email goes out within 60 seconds. The competitor hasn't even seen the lead yet."
4. "Fixed price, fixed timeline, no surprises. $500. 7 days. Done."
5. "I remove my own access within 24 hours of delivery. You own everything."
6. "The health check runs every morning at 8 AM. If something breaks overnight, you know before the day starts."
7. "Before I send anything, I run a full end-to-end test with dummy data. Nothing goes to you without passing that test."
8. "This isn't a template you rent. This is a custom system built for your tools and your workflow."
9. "The Miro board shows you exactly what gets built before I build anything. You approve it first."
10. "Set it up once — it runs forever. Save hours every week without hiring anyone new."

**Three rules for that tone:**
- Statements end going DOWN in pitch — not up. Falling pitch = confidence.
- Short sentences. One idea. Full stop. Then the next one.
- Connected speech: gonna, wanna, lemme, gotta — not word-by-word.

## The 5-Day Sprint (Do This First)

**Rule:** Every session ends with a recording. Listening back is mandatory — it's your only feedback loop.
**Rule:** Every drill uses real sentences from `06-automation-context.md` and `07-fullstack-context.md`. No generic examples.

### Day 1 — Falling Intonation + Sentence Breaks
**Layer:** Statements sound like statements. Short, clean sentences.

- Read `01-accent-drills.md` → Sound 5 (Falling Pitch) — say all drills 5x
- Read `02-grammar-drills.md` → Two-Second Rule + "Which Is" Ban — do all correction exercises
- Take the 10 north star sentences above — say each with falling pitch, 2-second pause after each
- **End recording:** Say the 10 north star sentences. Play back. If any rises at the end, redo it.

**Pass:** All 10 statements fall in pitch. No chaining.

### Day 2 — Connected Speech + American Replacements
**Layer added on top of Day 1.**

- Read `01-accent-drills.md` → Sound 4 (Connected Speech) — all 7 business drills
- Read `03-replacements.md` — all 10 categories, say each replacement 5x
- Take the audit call opening from `06-automation-context.md` — deliver it with reductions
- **End recording:** 60-second Loom intro using gonna/wanna/lemme. All statements still fall.

**Pass:** 4+ natural reductions. No robotic word-by-word delivery.

### Day 3 — Discovery Questions
**Layer added on top of Days 1–2.**

- Read `02-grammar-drills.md` → The Question Flip — all examples
- Read `05-scenarios.md` → Scenario 3 (30 discovery questions) — say each 3x
- Use the 5 diagnosis questions from `06-automation-context.md` — say each 5x
- **End recording:** Full 90-second audit call opening (opening → 5 questions).

**Pass:** Every question has auxiliary verb before subject. Zero "What your process look like?" patterns.

### Day 4 — Tense Precision
**Layer added on top of Days 1–3.**

- Read `05-scenarios.md` → Scenario 1 (Recapping Past) + Scenario 2 (Planning Future)
- Build a Marcus recap (past tense locked) and a Diana plan (future tense locked)
- Say each 5x. Record. One tense per paragraph — no mixing.
- **End recording:** Marcus recap then Diana plan, back to back.

**Pass:** Zero tense-switching in any 30-second stretch. Client can clearly hear "this happened" vs "this will happen."

### Day 5 — Full Integration (The Audit Call)
**All layers running simultaneously.**

- Read `05-scenarios.md` → Scenario 4, Scripts A, B, C — say each from start to finish, no stopping
- Do the complete audit call from `06-automation-context.md`: opening → 5 questions → Miro walkthrough → close
- Record the full call (4–5 minutes). Score it against the 6-check table below.

**Call-Ready Scoring Table:**

| Check | Pass | Fix |
|-------|------|-----|
| All statements fall in pitch | Yes / No | Redo Day 1 drills |
| Questions: auxiliary verb first, every time | Yes / No | Redo Day 3 drills |
| Zero tense-switching per 30-sec stretch | Yes / No | Redo Day 4 drills |
| 4+ connected speech reductions per minute | Yes / No | Redo Day 2 drills |
| Zero chains over 2 clauses | Yes / No | Two-Second Rule drill |
| 10 north star phrases — no hesitation | Yes / No | Say them daily |

**Score 5 or more → Call-ready. Score 4 or fewer → Target the failing areas for one more day.**

## Practice Principles

1. **Structures, not memorization.** You don't need to know the 10 sentences like your name. You need to know the PATTERN — one idea, full stop, falling pitch — so you can build any sentence with that pattern.

2. **Describe. Answer. Question. Explain.** Those are the four moves. Every real conversation is one of those four. Your five scenario scripts cover all of them.

3. **Mouth open, not eyes scanning.** Reading a sentence is not practice. Saying it aloud is practice. Recording it and listening back is feedback.

4. **Real content only.** Generic drills don't transfer. Every sentence you practice should be about n8n, webhooks, a client problem, a full-stack scenario. If it's not something you'd actually say on a call, it's not worth drilling.

5. **One focus per session.** Don't try to fix accent, grammar, and vocabulary at once. Pick the layer for that day and drill it until it feels automatic.
