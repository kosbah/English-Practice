# American Replacements — Miami Style
## Stop Sounding Formal. Start Sounding Real.

> **How to use this file:**
> Pick one section per day. Read each phrase out loud 3 times.
> Stress the CAPITAL syllables. Then use the phrase in a real sentence about your work.
> Replace the Egyptian/formal version in your brain — permanently.

---

## 1. YES

| Don't Say | Say This Instead | Phonetic | Vibe |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|------|
| Yes | Yeah | [YEH] | Casual, natural |
| Yes, of course | A hundred percent | [uh HUN-dred per-SENT] | Strong agreement |
| Yes, exactly | Exactly. / Precisely. | [ig-ZAKT-lee] | Professional |
| Yes, sure | For sure. | [fer SHUR] | Relaxed confidence |
| Yes, I agree | Totally. / Absolutely. | [TOH-tuh-lee] | Enthusiastic |
| Yes, no problem | Not a problem. / Done. | [nawt uh PROB-lem] | Action-taker energy |

**Drill:** Say this out loud — "Yeah, a hundred percent — I can get that done by Thursday."
`[YEH, uh HUN-dred per-SENT — I kin get that DUN by THURZ-day]`

## 2. NO

| Don't Say | Say This Instead | Phonetic | Vibe |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|------|
| No | Nah | [NAH] | Casual |
| No, I can't | That's not gonna work. | [thats nawt GUH-nuh wurk] | Direct, no drama |
| No, this is wrong | That's off. Let me fix it. | [thats AWF — LET me fix it] | Accountable |
| No, I disagree | I see it differently. | [I SEE it DIF-rent-lee] | Professional push-back |
| No, not yet | Not yet — I'm still working on it. | [nawt YET] | Honest, in control |
| No, that's not possible | That's outside what we agreed on. | — | Boundary setting |

**Drill:** "Nah, that's not gonna work for this sprint — let me show you what does."

## 3. MAYBE

| Don't Say | Say This Instead | Phonetic | Vibe |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|------|
| Maybe | Could be. | [kud BEE] | Thoughtful |
| Maybe, I don't know | I'd have to look into it. | [Id HAV tuh luk IN-tuh it] | Professional |
| Maybe later | Let me get back to you on that. | [LET me get BAK tuh yoo] | Buying time, not avoiding |
| Maybe it works | It might — I'd want to test it first. | — | Analytical |
| Maybe yes, maybe no | I'm leaning toward yes, but let me confirm. | — | Honest and in control |

**Drill:** "I'd have to look into that — give me until end of day and I'll send you the answer."

## 4. SORRY

| Don't Say | Say This Instead | Phonetic | Vibe |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|------|
| Sorry | My bad. | [MY bad] | Quick, real |
| I'm sorry for the delay | That took longer than it should — my bad. | — | Accountable, not dramatic |
| I'm sorry, I made a mistake | I got that wrong — I'll fix it now. | — | Action > apology |
| Sorry to bother you | Quick question — | [KWIK KWEST-chun] | Confident, not apologetic |
| Sorry, I don't understand | I'm not following — can you say that again? | — | Clear, direct |

> **Key rule:** Americans respect accountability more than apology. Don't say "I'm so sorry."
> Say what went wrong + what you're doing about it. That's it.

**Drill:** "That took longer than it should — my bad. Here's where we are now and what I'm doing to catch up."

## 7. STARTING A SENTENCE

Use these to open statements. They signal confidence and control the room.

| Starter | Phonetic | When to Use |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Look — | [LUK] | You're about to say something important |
| Here's the thing — | [HEERZ thuh THING] | Explaining a reality or constraint |
| So basically — | [SOH BAY-sik-lee] | Simplifying something complex |
| Real talk — | [REEL TAWK] | Being direct and honest |
| The way I see it — | [thuh WAY I SEE it] | Giving your opinion |
| Here's what I'm thinking — | [HEERZ wut Im THINK-ing] | Proposing a direction |
| Let me be straight with you — | [LET mee bee STRAYT with yoo] | Setting expectations |
| Honestly — | [AW-nest-lee] | Authentic moment |

**Drill:** "Look — here's the thing. The system is ready but we need to test it before we go live. Here's what I'm thinking for the next 48 hours."

## 9. BUYING TIME (Replace "Um" and "Eh")

Never say "Um", "Ah", or "Eh" — pause instead, then use one of these.

| Instead of Um/Eh | Say | Phonetic |
|-----------------|-----|----------|
| Um... | (pause) So — | [SOH] |
| Eh... let me think | Right, so — | [RYTE, SOH] |
| Hmm... | Good question. (pause) | [GUD KWEST-chun] |
| I... um... | Here's what I'm thinking — | [HEERZ wut Im THINK-ing] |
| Let me... eh... | Give me one sec — | [GIV mee WUN SEK] |

> **The rule:** Silence is more professional than "um". Pause → breathe → start the sentence.

## 10. ENDING A CALL OR CONVERSATION

| Formal Version | American Version | Phonetic |
|----------------|-----------------|----------|
| Goodbye | Talk soon. | [TAWK soon] |
| Have a good day | Take care. | [tayk KAIR] |
| I will send it | I'll shoot that over. | [Ile SHOOT that OH-ver] |
| I am looking forward | Looking forward to it. | [LUK-ing FOR-werd tuh it] |
| We will continue | Let's keep the momentum going. | — |
| Thank you for this meeting | Appreciate the time — let's do it again. | — |

**Full closing drill:**
"Appreciate you jumping on — I'll shoot the proposal over tonight. Looking forward to moving on this. Talk soon."
