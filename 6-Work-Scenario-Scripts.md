# Work Scenario Scripts — Automation Freelancer
## 4 Scenarios. Real Business Context. Say It Until It's Yours.

> **How to use this file:**
> Each scenario has 30 sentences + a full drill. Say them aloud — don't just read.
> Use the phonetic stress markings (CAPITALS = stress). Record yourself.
> The goal: these sentences should come out automatically when you're on a real call.

---

# SCENARIO 1: RECAPPING PAST WORK
## "Here's What I Did" — Past Tense Mastery

> **Grammar anchor:** Lock into past tense. Every sentence ends before the next one starts.
> No "and then... and then... which is..." — each sentence is complete. Stop. New sentence.

### 30 Sentences — Say Each 3x

**Starting the recap:**
1. "So last WEEK I finished setting up the LEAD follow-up SE-quence for the client."
2. "I just wrapped up the first SPRINT — here's where we are."
3. "Let me walk you through what I built this week."
4. "Here's a quick recap of what happened since our last call."
5. "We made a LOT of progress this week — let me break it down."

**Describing what you built:**
6. "I set up the automation in MAKE — it triggers every time a new lead comes in."
7. "I connected the web form to AIRTABLE — all the data flows in automatically."
8. "I built the email SE-quence — five emails, spaced over ten DAYS."
9. "I wired up the WEBHOOK between the CRM and the email tool."
10. "I configured the CONDITIONAL LOGIC so leads get sorted by source."
11. "I integrated CLAUDE into the workflow — it generates the personalized follow-up."
12. "I deployed the NODE.js backend to handle the API calls."
13. "I finished the FLUTTER frontend — the admin DASH-board is live."

**Reporting results:**
14. "The open rate went from TWEN-tee percent to SEV-en-tee percent in the first week."
15. "Response time dropped from FOR-tee FIVE minutes to under TWO minutes."
16. "We processed FIVE HUN-dred leads last month — all automated."
17. "The client saved about TEN hours a week from day one."
18. "Three OUT of FIVE leads that went through the sequence booked a call."

**Handling problems:**
19. "We ran into an issue with the API — I fixed it within TWEN-tee FOUR hours."
20. "The WEBHOOK was failing — turned out to be an authentication error. I patched it."
21. "There was a delay in the email delivery — I reconfigured the SMTP settings."
22. "The client asked for a change mid-sprint — I adjusted the scope and updated the timeline."
23. "I caught a bug in the CONDITIONAL LOGIC — leads were going into the wrong bucket. Fixed."

**Closing a recap:**
24. "That's where we are — everything is running and the client is happy."
25. "Overall, the first sprint went well — we're on track."
26. "The foundation is solid — we can start building the inbox management module next."
27. "I documented everything in NOTION so the client can follow along."
28. "I sent the summary over YESTERDAY — let me know if you have questions."
29. "The build is at about AY-tee percent — we wrap up by END of WEEK."
30. "Bottom line: the system works. The leads are being followed up. The client is seeing results."

### Scenario 1 Drill
Describe what you actually worked on today — in EXACTLY 5 sentences. Past tense only.
No "and then". Each sentence stops completely before the next one starts.

Example:
"I finished the webhook integration between the form and Airtable. The data is flowing correctly. I tested it with ten dummy leads. All ten showed up in the database within two seconds. I'll document the setup tomorrow morning."

# SCENARIO 2: PLANNING FOR THE FUTURE
## "Here's What We're Going to Do" — Future Tense Mastery

> **Grammar anchor:** Use "I'm going to", "we're going to", "I'll", "we'll", "once X, then Y".
> Lock into future. Don't mix past and future in the same sentence.

### 30 Sentences — Say Each 3x

**Opening a plan:**

3. "I want to take you through what's coming next — step by STEP."
4. "Here's the roadmap. Three PHASES. I'll explain each one."
5. "The next MOVE is to build out the inbox management MODULE."

**What you will build:**
6. "I'm GUH-nuh connect your CRM to AIRTABLE so your team can see the data in real TIME."
7. "We're GUH-nuh set up a SE-quence that fires within TWO minutes of a lead coming in."
8. "I'll build the DASHBOARD so you can see every lead's STATUS at a glance."
9. "We're GUH-nuh add CLAUDE to the workflow — it'll write the follow-up emails for you."
10. "I'm GUH-nuh integrate GMAIL so your inbox is fully managed by the automation."
11. "Once the LEAD sequence is done, we'll move to the CONTENT publishing workflow."
12. "I'll set up the WEBHOOK first — that's the foundation everything else runs on."
13. "We're GUH-nuh build the CONDITIONAL LOGIC so each lead gets the right message."

**Timeline and delivery:**
14. "I can have the first version LIVE within FOR-tee AYT hours."
15. "The full build will take about a MONTH from kickoff."
16. "I'll send you a progress update every FRIDAY."
17. "We're targeting END of KYOO WUN for the full deployment."
18. "Once I finish the backend, I'll hand you the credentials and walk you through it."
19. "In TWO sprints we'll have all THREE use cases running."
20. "I'll send the proposal TONIGHT — we can review it on our next call."

**Setting expectations:**
21. "Here's what I'll need from you: access to the CRM and the form BUILDER."
22. "One thing to keep in mind — the integration takes about TWEN-tee FOUR hours to stabilize."
23. "I want to test this with TEN real leads before we call it LIVE."
24. "If anything needs to change, I'll flag it early — no surprises."
25. "I'm GUH-nuh build this in phases so you can see results at every STEP."

**Closing a plan:**
26. "That's the PLAN. Any questions before I start?"
27. "I'll have something to show you by END of WEEK."
28. "We're GUH-nuh get this right — I've built this exact system THREE times before."
29. "Once this is running, you won't have to touch it. It handles itself."
30. "Bottom line: in THIRTY DAYS you'll have a fully automated lead machine."

### Scenario 2 Drill
Explain your next 3 steps on any current project — in exactly 3 sentences. Future tense only.

Example:
"I'm going to finish the Node.js backend by tomorrow night. Then I'll connect it to the Flutter dashboard and run the first end-to-end test. Once that passes, we go live."

# SCENARIO 3: ASKING DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
## "Tell Me About Your Business" — Question Mastery

> **Grammar anchor:** Every question needs an auxiliary verb BEFORE the subject.
> "Do you have..." NOT "You have...?"
> "What does your process look like?" NOT "What your process look like?"

### 30 Questions — Say Each 3x

**Understanding their current situation:**
1. "What does your CURRENT follow-up process look like?"
2. "How do you handle NEW leads right now — is it manual?"
3. "Do you have a CRM, or are you tracking things in a SPREADSHEET?"
4. "How many leads are you getting per MONTH?"
5. "How long does it typically take your TEAM to respond to a new lead?"
6. "What platforms are you currently using for OUTREACH?"
7. "Are you on ANY automation tools right now, or starting from scratch?"
8. "How does a lead currently make it from your FORM to your INBOX?"

**Understanding their pain:**
9. "What's the BIGGEST bottleneck in your sales process right now?"
10. "How many leads do you think you're LOSING because of slow follow-up?"
11. "What does a MISSED lead cost you on average?"
12. "Is your TEAM spending time on things that could be automated?"
13. "What would a SEV-en-tee percent reduction in manual work mean for you?"
14. "If you didn't have to touch the INBOX at all — what would you do with that time?"
15. "What's the MOST frustrating part of managing your leads right now?"

**Understanding their tech stack:**
16. "What CRM are you using — HUBSPOT, SALESFORCE, something custom?"
17. "How are you currently capturing LEADS — web form, landing page, DMs?"
18. "Do you have access to the API for your current TOOLS?"
19. "Are you on GOOGLE Workspace or Microsoft?"
20. "What does your CONTENT workflow look like right now?"

**Understanding their goals:**
21. "What does SUCCESS look like for you in the NEXT ninety days?"
22. "If we could AUTOMATE one thing right now, what would have the BIGGEST impact?"
23. "Are you looking for a QUICK WIN or a long-term system build?"
24. "How quickly do you need this to be UP and running?"
25. "What's your BUDGET range for this project?"

**Deepening the conversation:**
26. "Can you walk me through EXACTLY what happens when a lead comes in today?"
27. "When you say the FOLLOW-UP isn't working — what specifically breaks down?"
28. "What have you ALREADY tried to fix this?"
29. "Who on your team would be using this SYSTEM day to day?"
30. "What would make this the BEST investment you made this year?"

### Scenario 3 Drill
Pick any topic (lead generation, email management, content publishing).
Generate 10 questions about it. Say them all aloud. Record.
Then answer 3 of them — as if you're the client.

# SCENARIO 4: EXPLAINING YOUR SYSTEM
## "Let Me Walk You Through This" — 3 Full Scripts

> These are 3-minute scripts. Say them aloud, start to finish.
> Stress the CAPITALS. Use connected speech. End every statement with falling pitch.

---

## Script A: Lead Follow-Up Automation — VSL Style
*Use this for: pitches, intro calls, video explanations*

"Look — if you're getting leads but not CLOSING them, the problem isn't your OFFER. The problem is your FOLLOW-UP.

Here's the reality: sixty percent of deals close after the FIFTH follow-up. But most businesses stop after ONE.

Why? Because FOLLOW-UP is manual. It's time-consuming. And nobody on your team has the BANDWIDTH to do it right.

That's where I come IN.

I build automation systems that follow up with EVERY lead — automatically — within TWO minutes of them hitting your FORM.

Here's how the SYS-tem works.

Step ONE: A lead fills out your form — whether that's a web form, a landing PAGE, a DM, doesn't MATTER.

Step TWO: The automation CAPTURES that lead, saves their data to your DATABASE, and routes them to the right SE-quence based on their answers.

Step THREE: Within TWO minutes, they get a personalized response — powered by CLAUDE from Anthropic. It knows who they ARE, what they asked for, and what to say NEXT.

Step FOUR: Over the next TEN days, they get a SE-quence of emails that NURTURES them toward a call. All automatic. All personalized. None of it needs your TEAM.

Step FIVE: Every interaction gets LOGGED in your CRM. Your team sees exactly where each lead IS at all times.

The result? Your response time goes from FORTY-FIVE minutes to under TWO. Your open rate goes from twenty percent to SEVENTY. And your CLOSE rate goes up — because you're actually following UP.

I've built this SYS-tem three times. It works.

If you're ready to stop losing leads to slow FOLLOW-UP — shoot me a message and let's get STARTED."

## Script B: Email Inbox Management — Audit Call Style
*Use this for: showing a live system to a new client*

"Okay, so let me show you what I BUILT.

This is your INBOX — right now, you're getting about two hundred emails a DAY. Your team is manually sorting them, responding to the URGENT ones, and ignoring the rest. That's about three hours of work every DAY — gone.

Here's what the automation does.

When an email comes IN — right here — the SYS-tem reads it using the CLAUDE API. It categorizes it in under ONE second: is this a new lead, an existing client, a support request, or NOISE?

If it's a new LEAD — it automatically gets added to your CRM and triggers the FOLLOW-UP se-quence we built. No manual entry. No delay.

If it's an existing CLIENT — the system pulls their HISTORY, drafts a response based on your communication STYLE, and puts it in your drafts folder for a one-click SEND. You review it in ten seconds. Done.

If it's SUPPORT — it gets routed to the right person on your team with the CONTEXT already attached.

If it's NOISE — unsubscribes, newsletters, automated emails — it gets ARCHIVED. You never see it.

What used to take three HOURS a day now takes FIFTEEN minutes. Your team reviews the drafts, hits send, and moves ON.

Everything is LOGGED. Everything is TRACKABLE. And if you ever need to AUDIT a conversation, it's all in the DASHBOARD — right HERE.

That's the system. Questions before we talk about SETUP?"

## Script C: Content Publishing Workflow — Technical Handoff Style
*Use this for: explaining to a developer, a technical client, or a team member*

"Alright, let me walk you through the ARCHITECTURE.

The CONTENT publishing workflow has three main LAYERS.

LAYER one is the INPUT layer. Content comes in from three sources: a NOTION database where you draft posts, a GOOGLE DOCS folder for long-form content, and a VOICE MEMO transcription pipeline for ideas you record on the go.

LAYER two is the PROCESSING layer. This is where CLAUDE does the work. When a new piece of content gets FLAGGED as 'ready to publish', the automation pulls it into the PIPELINE. Claude formats it for each PLATFORM — LinkedIn post, Twitter thread, email newsletter — based on templates we built together. It adds the right HASHTAGS, adjusts the TONE for each channel, and generates a thumbnail DESCRIPTION if needed.

LAYER three is the OUTPUT layer. Processed content gets pushed to a SCHEDULING tool — we're using BUFFER right now, but we can swap it for any PLATFORM with an API. Each piece goes out at the OPTIMAL time based on your audience's engagement DATA.

The whole thing runs on MAKE. The webhook fires whenever you update the STATUS field in Notion from 'draft' to 'ready'. You don't touch anything after that.

One thing to NOTE: the CLAUDE API calls add about THREE seconds to the processing time per piece of content. That's fine for async publishing. If you need real-time posting, we'd move that to a QUEUE.

Any questions on the FLOW before I show you the actual MAKE scenario?"
