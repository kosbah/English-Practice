## Weekly Self-Scoring

Answer these 5 questions every Saturday. Be honest. This is your feedback loop.

**1. Tense control:** Did I switch tenses mid-story this week?
- [ ] No — locked it
- [ ] A few times — which situations?
- [ ] Often — drill Scenarios 1 and 2 more next week

**2. Sentence chaining:** Did I use "and... and... which is..." this week?
- Count: _____ times
- Target: under 3 per day by Week 4, 0 by Week 8

**3. Direct translation:** Did I say an Egyptian phrase in English structure?
- What was it? (write it down)
- What's the correct version? (look it up, write it down, say it 10x)

**4. Recording time:** How many minutes did I record myself this week?
- _____ minutes (target: 30 minutes/week minimum)

**5. American replacements:** Which phrase from File 4 did I actually use this week?
- Write it here: _____________________
- Did it feel natural? Y / N

## The 3 Rules That Make This Work

**Rule 1: Mouth open, not eyes.**
Reading the sentences is not practicing. Saying them out loud is practicing. If your mouth isn't moving, it doesn't count.

**Rule 2: Record, don't just speak.**
Your brain thinks you sound fluent. The recording tells the truth. Record at least 3x per week. Play it back. Find one thing to fix.

**Rule 3: One thing per session.**
Don't try to fix your accent, grammar, and vocabulary at the same time. Pick today's focus. Do it fully. Move on.

## What to Do When You're Stuck Mid-Sentence

This will happen. Here's the protocol:

1. **Pause.** Don't say "um". Don't fill the silence. Pause.
2. **Buy time.** Say: "Here's what I'm thinking—" or "Good question—" or "Let me put it this way—"
3. **Simplify.** Say a shorter version of what you wanted to say. Short and clear beats long and tangled.
4. **Move on.** Don't apologize. Don't explain that you got stuck. Just keep going.

The client doesn't know what you were going to say. Only you do.
