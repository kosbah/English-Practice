English Training Vocabulary

🔹 2️⃣0️⃣1️⃣ More Like X Than Y

Meaning: A framework used to reframe a situation, pointing out that the current reality doesn't match the original pitch.
Example: “Straight up, dealing with this client feels more like babysitting than running a B2B agency.”

🔹 2️⃣0️⃣2️⃣ Lead

Meaning: A warm body with a pulse, a pain point, and a credit card; the raw fuel for your sales engine.
Example: “If someone drops their email in your opt-in form, they stop being traffic and officially become a lead.”

🔹 2️⃣0️⃣3️⃣ Lead Generation

Meaning: The relentless, systematized hunt to fill your pipeline with high-quality prospects before you run out of cash flow.
Example: “The entire front-end of the website is just a trap optimized for lead generation.”

🔹 2️⃣0️⃣4️⃣ Completion Rate

Meaning: The brutal metric that separates the serious buyers from the tire-kickers who abandon your funnel halfway.
Example: “If 100 people click the VSL but only 60 book a call, your completion rate is 60%—we need to optimize that.”

🔹 2️⃣0️⃣5️⃣ Tradeoff

Meaning: The opportunity cost you eat; what you have to sacrifice to secure a bigger, more strategic win.
Example: “Bootstrapping gives you 100% equity, but the tradeoff is you scale 10x slower than VC-backed founders.”

🔹 2️⃣0️⃣6️⃣ Gets Expensive Fast

Meaning: Burning through your runway at lightning speed because you didn't calculate the backend costs.
Example: “Running broad Facebook ads without a dialed-in pixel gets expensive fast.”

🔹 2️⃣0️⃣7️⃣ Tight

Meaning: Running lean, bootstrapped, and protective of every single dollar of operating capital.
Example: “The budget is tight this month, so cut the dead weight and only focus on revenue-generating tasks.”

🔹 2️⃣0️⃣8️⃣ Free Plan Limits Are Tight

Meaning: The aggressive paywall squeeze SaaS companies use to force free users to pull out their Amex.
Example: “The free plan limits are tight on purpose; you hit the ceiling in three days and have to upgrade to Premium.”

🔹 2️⃣0️⃣9️⃣ Scrappy

Meaning: Bootstrapped, relentless, and willing to out-work the competition using pure grit instead of a massive budget.
Example: “They’re a scrappy startup—no fancy office, just four killers in a Discord server printing cash.”

🔹 2️⃣1️⃣0️⃣ Underdog

Example: “We were the underdog pitching against massive agencies, but our hunger won the account.”

🔹 2️⃣1️⃣1️⃣ Scrappy Underdog

Example: “That company is a scrappy underdog; they have zero red tape and can ship features overnight.”

🔹 2️⃣1️⃣3️⃣ Genuinely
