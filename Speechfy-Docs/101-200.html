English Training Vocabulary

🔹 1️⃣0️⃣1️⃣ Multiple Six Figures a Month

Meaning: The elite tier of agency owners and infopreneurs pulling in $200k+ monthly; the point where you stop worrying about cash flow and start worrying about wealth preservation.
Example: “Once we dialed in the paid ads and the backend email flows, the business scaled to multiple six figures a month on autopilot.”

🔹 1️⃣0️⃣2️⃣ Off the Hamster Wheel

Meaning: Escaping the 9-to-5 matrix or the brutal freelance trap of trading hours for dollars, usually by building scalable systems or locking in massive leverage.
Example: “Securing that 20% rev-share deal with a massive creator finally got him off the hamster wheel.”

🔹 1️⃣0️⃣3️⃣ Client Selection

Meaning: Ruthlessly vetting prospects to ensure you only onboard high-ticket whales with capital, audience, and a proven offer, avoiding the brokies who drain your energy.
Example: “Good client selection means rejecting the headache accounts and only partnering with founders who are already printing cash.”

🔹 1️⃣0️⃣4️⃣ Mediocre

Meaning: NGMI (Not Gonna Make It); doing just enough to exist but lacking the killer instinct to dominate the market.
Example: “A mediocre operator can still print money if they plug into a client who has insane organic traffic.”

🔹 1️⃣0️⃣5️⃣ Reputation

Meaning: Your brand equity; the invisible asset that allows you to charge premium, high-ticket prices without getting price-shopped.
Example: “A strong reputation in the B2B space makes founders beg you to take their $10k retainer.”

🔹 1️⃣0️⃣6️⃣ Positioned

Meaning: Architecting your personal brand so you are viewed as the absolute category king, not just another commodity in a saturated market.
Example: “She’s positioned as the undisputed go-to authority for scaling Airbnb portfolios to 8 figures.”

🔹 1️⃣0️⃣7️⃣ Uncommitted

Meaning: Tire-kickers with a scarcity mindset who want the millionaire result but refuse to put skin in the game.
Example: “Selling a $50 course attracts uncommitted buyers; selling a $5k mastermind attracts killers.”

🔹 1️⃣0️⃣8️⃣ Cocky

Meaning: Radiating unapologetic alpha energy; having a massive ego that is entirely justified by a lethal track record of closing deals.
Example: “He sounded cocky on the discovery call, but his Stripe dashboard had the receipts to back it up.”

🔹 1️⃣0️⃣9️⃣ Deeply Researched

Meaning: Stalking a prospect's entire digital footprint to weaponize their pain points and pitch a hyper-targeted solution.
Example: “A deeply researched cold DM calls out their exact bottleneck and offers a plug-and-play fix.”

🔹 1️⃣1️⃣0️⃣ Custom VSL

Meaning: A bespoke Video Sales Letter engineered with hypnotic copy to convert cold traffic into high-ticket buyers for a specific offer.
Example: “I recorded a custom VSL that isolated his target audience's deepest fears and pitched the ultimate transformation.”

🔹 1️⃣1️⃣1️⃣ Mockup

Meaning: The high-fidelity prototype or wireframe you flash in a pitch to prove you can execute the vision.
Example: “I built a sleek funnel mockup on Figma to visually close the client before he even saw the invoice.”

🔹 1️⃣1️⃣2️⃣ Close Clients

Meaning: Getting the prospect to stop stalling, pull out the Amex, and sign the legally binding docs.
Example: “The objection handling was flawless, and I closed the client on a $5k monthly retainer.”

🔹 1️⃣1️⃣3️⃣ Articulate

Meaning: Pitching with lethal precision; the ability to verbally dominate a room and explain the ROI better than the founder can.
Example: “He articulated the bottleneck in their sales process so perfectly that they practically begged to hire him.”

🔹 1️⃣1️⃣4️⃣ Conviction

🔹 1️⃣1️⃣5️⃣ Backed by Logic
